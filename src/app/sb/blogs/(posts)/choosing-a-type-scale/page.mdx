# Choosing a type scale that survives real content

<p className="byline">10 July 2026 · Shine</p>

Type scales look effortless in demos with three headings and a paragraph. Then
real content arrives: long German compound words, data tables, timestamps,
legal footnotes — and the scale falls apart.

A few principles that have held up for me:

- **Fewer steps than you think.** Most products need five or six sizes, not
  twelve.
- **Test with tabular data early.** Numbers expose bad line-height choices
  faster than prose.
- **Pin the body size first.** Build the scale outward from 16px, not downward
  from the hero.
